General Information

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to machine learning with a specific focus on its applications in construction research. Throughout the course, students will develop a strong foundation in machine learning principles and techniques, enabling them to apply this knowledge to solve real-world problems in the construction industry. The course is designed for students with a basic understanding of programming and mathematics, but no prior knowledge of machine learning is required.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental concepts and types of machine learning.
  • Apply supervised learning techniques for regression and classification problems.
  • Utilize unsupervised learning algorithms for clustering and dimensionality reduction.
  • Evaluate and select appropriate models using model selection and evaluation techniques.
  • Perform feature engineering to enhance the performance of machine learning models.
  • Identify and mitigate bias in machine learning algorithms.
  • Gain an introduction to deep learning and its applications.
  • Apply machine learning to predict construction time and cost, and optimize schedules.
  • Explore real-world case studies and examples of machine learning in construction.

Syllabus

  1. 1. Introduction to machine learning
  2. 2. Supervised learning for regression and classification
  3. 3. Unsupervised learning for clustering and dimensionality reduction
  4. 4. Model selection and evaluation
  5. 5. Feature engineering
  6. 6. Bias and fairness in machine learning
  7. 7. Introduction to deep learning
  8. 8. Applications of machine learning in construction research
  9. 9. Real-world examples and case studies

Additionally, real-world examples and case studies will be presented to reinforce the concepts learned throughout the course.

Grading Scheme

The course grading will be based on a combination of quizzes, assignments, and a final project. The breakdown of the grading scheme is as follows:

Quizzes: Regular quizzes will be conducted at the end of each module to assess students' understanding of the material covered. These quizzes will contribute to 30% of the final grade.

Assignments: Students will complete assignments that involve applying machine learning techniques to real-world construction research problems. These assignments will allow students to practice their skills and demonstrate their understanding of the concepts taught in the course. Assignments will account for 70% of the final grade.

The course provides a comprehensive learning experience with a balance of theoretical concepts, practical applications, and real-world case studies. By the end of the course, students will have a strong foundation in machine learning and be well-prepared to apply these techniques to address challenges in the construction industry.
